The Dimensional Change Card Sort (DCCS) test is a widely-used measure of cognitive flexibility and executive function, particularly in young children (ages 3-7). Participants sort cards first by one dimension (e.g., color) and then must switch to sorting by a different dimension (e.g., shape). The test assesses the ability to flexibly shift between mental sets, inhibit prepotent responses, and maintain new sorting rules in working memory.

The test has three phases:
1. Color Game: Sort cards by color (blue vs. red)
2. Shape Game: Sort cards by shape (rabbit vs. boat), requiring a dimensional shift
3. Border Game (advanced): Switch between color and shape rules based on card border (if passed shape game)

Performance on the DCCS is a strong indicator of executive function development. Children under age 4 typically perseverate on the initial sorting rule and struggle with the dimensional shift, while older children successfully switch rules.

This implementation includes optional audio narration for child-friendly administration and can be configured for different difficulty levels.
